What is AEC ?


The Algerian Engineering Competition gathers students, fresh graduates, and tech enthusiasts from all over the country to work on solving technical and industrial problems, submitted by various companies. Its main goal is to fuel innovation and enable teams to make something remarkable in 6 or 7 days in total. It allows participants to showcase their ingenuity in subjects not only limited to coding and software projects but ranging through all fields of engineering!

Course of the event


The competition is divided into two phases :

Phase one : 2nd, 3rd and 4th of December

It will take place in the 6 wilayas : Algiers, Constantine, Oran, Tlemcen, Skikda and Bechar.
Each team will be oriented to the closest wilaya to compete.
They will be given three sub-problematics in three different categories : wildfires, lack of water resources, and healthcare problems during the pandemic.
First day will be dedicated to teaming up, workshops, and statement scoops reveal, after everything is set, eligible teams might start working on their ideas. The contestants have 48 hours to come up with a solution and present their prototype for the jury.
Participants will have direct contact with their mentors using Discord, in order to discuss and structure their ideas. Workshops will be provided through all this phase by mentors.
Fifteen teams will be selected to participate in phase II.


Phase two : 9th , 10th and 11th of December

The finale will take place in Algiers.
The fifteen selected teams will complete the projects they started in phase I. The contestants will be given 2 days to prepare.
Same as the previous phase, Workshops will be provided by mentors and experts.
Competitors must present their projects to a jury of experts and will be evaluated on different criteria such as practicality, execution and originality.
There will be three winning teams, one in each category.

Our Themes

1- Wildfires:

Wildfires affect many different segments of the population. Impacts range from injuries, or even death, to property loss or damage, from dislocation to temporary or long-term health impacts, both directly or indirectly. Despite wildfires being a yearly occurrence in Algeria, the country has not experienced such devastation in its recent history. This year’s catastrophe showed the country’s vulnerability to these kinds of disasters. Since 9 August 2021, 90 people have been killed including 57 civilians and 33 soldiers. The Algerian authorities now need to take several steps to prevent the repeat of this tragedy in the future and find the right solutions for a better Algeria.

2- Lack of water resources :

Only 3% of the world’s water is drinkable and water scarcity affects more than 40% of people, an alarming figure that is projected to rise as temperatures do. More and more countries are experiencing water stress, including Algeria. Water quality, treatment, protection, and development are framed by problems related to water sustainability. This question of water resources is posed urgently in Algeria, underground water resources have been greatly reduced and efforts in this area remain very limited and often without achieving tangible results. Moreover, while underground water is reaching dangerously low levels, the demand for water from the urban people, industry, and especially agriculture is rapidly increasing, so complicating the problem, natural resources are at the core of its sustainable development and are critical to socio-economic growth. Therefore, at the end of the 21st century, Algeria will find itself in a very critical situation.

3- Health care problems:

The coronavirus disease has caused increasing challenges for healthcare professionals globally. Since social distancing is the most effective way to contain the outspread of this virus, it is not easy to implement for healthcare professionals who require direct contact with COVID-19 patients and puts them at a high risk of being infected themselves. As in many other countries, the Algerian Government implemented intermittent, partial lockdowns and curfews, as well as travel cancellation and isolation of returning citizens to promote physical distancing, decrease the spread of the virus, flatten the curve of infections, and allow hospitals more time to prepare. Despite these measures, COVID-19 continued to spread, infecting 205.000 and killing 5 864 people. As the rate of daily infections exponentially increased, the effects of the pandemic extended to disturb all aspects of society. Rising poverty, unemployment, and isolation are matters of concern for many Algerians, along with worries surrounding worsening mental health and increasing domestic violence incidents as families are confined at home. Although Algeria's healthcare system ranks among the best in Africa, it lags far behind the standards of wealthier countries.

Participation criteria

Student or graduate ?

Whether you are a student or a graduate, you are welcome to participate in AEC!

Limited age ?

The required age to participate is 18 years old or above.

Team competition?

Yes, you must be in a team. The teams must have between 3 and 5 members.
Competitors who are registred without a team are obliged to team up on the first day. Otherwise, they will get excluded.
